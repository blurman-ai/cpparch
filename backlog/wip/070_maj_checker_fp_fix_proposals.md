## TL;DR — what we'll actually do, what we won't

**We do (P0, cheap, deterministic, no libclang):** attribution to git-hunk + blame,
git-rename/move/whole-file suppression, span coordinate revalidation, anchoring the window
to function boundaries (by brace balance), a joint token∧order gate. This hits the
**mechanical** FP classes (`other`, `whole-file`, `generated`, part of `coincidental`)
and barely requires semantics.

**We do carefully (P1, must measure against 195 TP):** file-local frequency down-weight
as a **precision-re-rank** (NOT a recall gate — we've already proven that idf inverts
ranking on recall, see §0), data-table/boilerplate classifiers, min-substantive-core.

**We won't do (outside fast-backend / contradicts the 2026-06-01 decision):** tree-sitter/AST
function-spans, control-flow/statement-shape skeletons as a load-bearing gate (needs a parser/AST;
#052 was removed, we're not going back), hundreds of framework stop-lists (Qt/JUCE/CUDA/…), driving
idiom/coincidental to zero (the TP/FP distribution overlap is proven — that's a floor, not a bug).

**Honest ceiling:** mechanical gates (P0) give precision ~32%→~45%. To reach 60%+ you must
hit `idiom`/`coincidental` — and that is exactly the proven floor; only
LLM-confirm (#059 L3) reaches there, and only in gate mode. Detailed FP budget — §5.

## 0. Reality-check — architecture vs. review (READ FIRST)

The review and the raw backlog (Appendix A) are written as if archcheck had a multi-layered
AST-aware / git-aware / multi-bucket pipeline with a parser. **It does not.** Per the
2026-06-01 decision ([docs/duplication_architecture.md](../../docs/duplication_architecture.md)):

- **A single detector — the token-based #056**, parser-free, fast-backend, **without libclang or
  `compile_commands.json`**. The #053 (line) and #052 (AST cross-TU) layers are **removed from the tree,
  we're not going back.** Any review item resting on "the scanner already has function-spans
  from clang" / "let's take an AST skeleton" is **inapplicable as is**: we have no AST.
- **idf weighting is already measured as HARMFUL on the recall gate** (§3.4 of the arch doc): on the headline case
  "operator flipped on every line", the rarest tokens diverge, idf gives them max weight,
  and **inverts** the ranking (a false switch 0.45 above the true copy 0.36). idf is appropriate
  ONLY for precision-re-rank, not for recall. **This directly contradicts** the most frequent
  backlog proposal ("file-local IDF", repeated dozens of times) — it is NOT a silver bullet.
- **The entropy/diversity guard is already measured with distribution overlap** between TP/FP (FP 0.25–0.28,
  true TP 0.32) — no clean formal threshold exists. The idiom-FP floor is **not a bug
  but a boundary of the method** (§9 of the arch doc).

**What of the review's "P0" we ALREADY have** (so — don't redo, at most fine-tune):

| Review proposal | Status in #056 |
|---|---|
| Strip comments before tokenization (Task 6) | the lexer already cleans up; raw-string→`lit`, dead `#if 0` dropped. Check coverage of `//` / `/* */` / doxygen. |
| Joint token + ordered gate (Task 5) | **present and load-bearing** — token-LCS confirm is mandatory, calibration gate ~0.80 (§3.5). This is exactly "don't trust the token-bag without order". |
| Keep call-targets, don't normalize everything to `id` | **present — a key decision** (selective normalization, §6). Matches the SourcererCC philosophy (see §2). A cheap replacement for "call-target Jaccard" (review P2.1). |
| Vendor/generated exclude (Task 9) | **in progress** — #068 (vendor), #069 (single-file lib), §8 (banner/signals). |
| Data-table suppression (Task 7) | **partial** — raw-string blobs collapsed; the numeric data-heavy guard is NOT yet implemented. |

Conclusion: **not "implement 130 rules from scratch", but (a) fine-tune the already load-bearing phases and
(b) add 4–5 mechanical gates that genuinely don't exist.**

## 2. External consensus (prior art, web) — what the "grown-ups" think

Good news: our FP classes and our decisions are **canonical**, not amateur improvisation.

- **SourcererCC** (Sajnani et al., arXiv 1512.06448) deliberately **does NOT normalize
  identifier names**, to preserve precision, and uses **token-ordering filters** for
  pruning. → Directly confirms our **selective normalization** (keep callee names) and
  **token-LCS order gate** as state-of-the-art, not a hack.
- **Canonical Type-3 FP causes** in the literature (benchmark surveys): "very short
  sequences of assignments; different API calls repeated in switch case branches;
  extremely long single statements with string concatenation". → **Exact match** with
  our top classes (`idiom` = guard/switch ladders, `data-table` = string-concat/init).
  This is a known hard floor for EVERYONE, not an archcheck defect.
- **NiCad** (Cordy & Roy, ICPC 2011): precision 89–96% — but **on artificial
  mutation clones** (BigCloneBench / Mutation Framework). Our 32% is on a **real**
  AI-drift corpus with a shared vocabulary. Our numbers **cannot** be compared with the "90%+" from papers:
  these are different (easier) measurements. The realistic ceiling on real code is lower.
- **Kapser & Godfrey** ("not all clones are harmful", a taxonomy of benign clones): the right answer is
  **classification/buckets + FP filtering by taxonomy**, and "a clone's usefulness is subjective, depends
  on the user's goal". → Confirms our **character classifier** (VERBATIM/RENAMED/
  EDITED) and the mode split (snapshot=explorer vs gate). The FP categories from their work —
  "list of assignments / list of declarations" — are our `data-table` and `header-impl`.
- **PMD CPD** already exposes knobs `ignore-literals` / `ignore-literal-sequences` /
  `ignore-identifiers` / skip-blocks. → Our data-table/literal classifiers are not exotic.
- **git rename/copy** (`git-blame -C`, `git diff -M -C`) — git detects move/copy **itself**,
  including copies in the commit that created the file. → whole-file/move suppression = **git-native**,
  not scoring.
- **Sonar (community, 2025):** complaints of dozens–hundreds of FPs per one real duplicate; if FPs
  are not suppressible/explainable — the rule gets **disabled**. → Confirms: at precision ~32%
  you can't keep a CI gate; you need (a) attribution, (b) explainability ("why it fired"),
  (c) confidence buckets.
- **TF-IDF in clone detection**: helps in combination with cosine ranking, but the modern
  precision gain comes not from raw token weight but from embeddings/ML. → Consistent with our
  "idf — for ranking, not for the gate; not a silver bullet".

§2 conclusion: the review is correct on prior art; **the main divergence from our reality is pipeline
scale** (we have a single token layer without AST) and **idf** (we have counter-data).

## 3. Realistic plan for our backend

### P0 — correctness/attribution (cheap, deterministic, no libclang)

Works in **commit/diff/baseline** modes (where "a code change happens" and a gate is in
place; §7 of the arch doc). In snapshot-explorer there's no git pair — but snapshot doesn't gate either.

1. **Diff-hunk + blame attribution.** A hit is valid only if the ADDED span crosses
   added/modified hunks of **this** commit; for a merge — diff against first-parent or
   attribute via blame. Removes `other` entirely and a chunk of `coincidental`
   (docs/chore/merge misattribution).
2. **git rename/move/whole-file suppression** before chunk-matching (`git diff -M -C`).
   BASE deleted + ADDED similar to the deleted BASE → move/refactor, not a duplicate. Removes `whole-file`.
3. **Coordinate revalidation.** Store `blob_sha,path,start,end,norm_span_hash`; before
   output, re-read the blob, re-tokenize, compare. Drop on mismatch. Removes phantom-range.
4. **Function-boundary anchoring** — strengthen the brace-balance fragmenter so the window doesn't
   capture the tail of one function + the head of the next. **Without a parser** (by the `)`→`{` signature).
5. **Symmetric-pair canonicalization** — (A,B) and (B,A) into one.
6. **Joint token∧order floor** — stop emitting `w high & line/LCS low`; set a
   joint threshold (exact numbers — fit on the #067 corpus, don't guess).

### P1 — low-signal classifiers (measure against 195 TP, otherwise recall drops)

1. **data-table/literal-run** classifier (numeric tables, `case X:return Y;` ladders,
   `push_back({…})` rows) — at the lexer level, as already done for raw-string.
2. **boilerplate-density** + **min substantive core** (≥N rare non-idiomatic tokens; floor
   ~10–15 significant non-brace lines; drop trivial forwarders).
3. **header-impl** gate (fraction of decl lines ≥0.7 and <2 executable → suppress; `.h`↔`.cpp`
   of the same basename requires high body overlap).
4. **file-local frequency down-weight** — **precision-re-rank after LCS only.** Before
   enabling: run against 195 TP, the TP-retention metric. If recall drops > threshold — revert.
   (We already have data that idf can hurt — that's why this is P1, not P0.)

### P2 — structural (expensive/unclear ROI) — only after P0/P1 measurements

- keyword-sequence skeleton-LCS as a **surrogate** for control-flow (the lexer keeps keywords) —
  research, may not pay off.
- LLM/semantic confirm (#059 L3) — final step, only in gate mode, where the verdict fixes/gates.

### Out of scope (honestly — we CAN'T / WON'T)

- **AST/tree-sitter function-spans, statement/CFG skeleton as a load-bearing gate** — no libclang
  in the fast-backend; #052 removed, the 2026-06-01 decision "we're not going back".
- **Hundreds of framework stop-lists** — replaced by selective-norm + file-local down-weight +
  min-core. Hard-coding Qt/JUCE/CUDA is a "heuristic swamp" (review §12 agrees).
- **idiom/coincidental → 0** — a proven floor (distribution overlap). We reduce, not zero out.

## 6. Output model (explainability — otherwise it gets disabled, see Sonar)

Not a flat list of "duplicates" — **confidence buckets** (partly already present: the character

## 7. Absorbed from #059 (governing principle + measurements + acceptance)

*#059 (coincidental/idiom-FP suppression) was merged here 2026-06-02 — it was a separate
framing of the same precision layer. What's unique from it:*

### Governing principle: confirm is justified ONLY by an action on the outcome
LLM-confirm (P2) costs tokens; its price is justified **only if the verdict leads to a
code change**, otherwise it's a dashboard (and archcheck per spec is a CI gate, not a dashboard).
Confirm must feed: **(a) a gate** (baseline freezes historical duplicates, CI fails a PR
on a new confirmed duplicate) or **(b) a remediation-hint** ("extract into a shared header/
base class", the Lakos vocabulary). The success metric = **"how much didn't grow / got fixed"**,
not "how much was found". A snapshot report without gate/remediation is an anti-goal.

### Measurement of selective normalization (the main lever, datavis-mw, precise@0.70)
| FP / TP | all-`id` | keep-calls+case |
|---|--:|--:|
| assign/call coincidental (WaterLevels↔EditorFrame) | 0.70 | **no pair** |
